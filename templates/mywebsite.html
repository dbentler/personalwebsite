Projects: My Webite

Why does it Exist?

Why do any of us exist? Jokes aside, I wanted a way to showcase myself online in a way that conveys both what I can do, and my personality (hence why I make some terrible jokes here and there). It allows recruiters to get a sense of what I'm capable of, and who I am, so they can have a better idea on if I'm a good fit for their company.

Of course, I also created it to expand my online footprint. When you google "Darren Bentler", all you get is my LinkedIn, some incorrect personal information, and some poems I wrote for AP Literature back in high school that were uploaded to my teacher's website.

What if there's someone who's searching for me and wants to get in contact? Perhaps an old friend or relative who lives in another country? If they find this website, they'll be able to find me and see what I've been up to. They'll also have several different ways of getting in contact with me. That's why this website is here.

I also wanted to experient with the Flask framework and web-design.

Website 2.0

Building off what I learned from 1.0

After not working on the website for a few weeks because of how ugly it was, I decided to do some soul searching on the internet. What I really wanted was just some inspiration on how I should layout the home page, as first impressions are important. I wanted the home page to be the quick reference guide. Something like, "Hey! Here's my skills. Here's what I've made and my experience. Here's how to contact me!".

I eventually stumbled upon a friend from high school's website, who is also a Software Developer. His name is Hans, and I was so impressed with his formatting I decided to replicate some parts of it. Hans, if you're reading this, your website saved mine from being a disgusting mess. If you're a recruiter who's now interested in Hans, I'm sorry to tell you but he's already taken by another corporation.

The Future of the Website

At the time of writing this, I'm currently working 8-hours a day on getting the website ready for launch (today is May 4th, 2021). As of right now, I do feel like it's ready, and I'm researching on what host I should use. I'm probably going to go with Google Apps, as it seems like the cheapest and easiest option out of the rest.

One thing that I don't really take advantage of is the fact that Flask is a dynamic framework. Right now, this site is completely static. I eventually want to make some sort of cryptography game inspired by the Cicada-3301 games on the site to challenge my friends with.

That being said, I have worked with MySQL before in a school assignment. It was part of that reason why I chose to go with Flask for this website.

I also want to eventually figure out a way to render *.md files on the site instead of having to write HTML, as it currently takes an hour+ to write a simple page. Not only that, but getting markdown files to render would help tremendously when I get around to writing about my other projects where I have to explain code (I'm currently looking into Flask-Markdown as solution).

Anyway, thank you for reading up about the history of the website. I hope you enjoyed the tale of how it came to be!
